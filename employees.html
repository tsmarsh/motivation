How to disrupt as an employee

Slow and steady (in a bad way)Work super slow. Use the wrong tools, like a tiny hammer when you need a big one. Take the long way around every task.

Find excuses to pauseTake forever when switching materials. Measure things way more than needed. Take extended bathroom breaks. “Forget” your tools so you have to go back for them.

Pretend you don’t get itEven if you understand instructions, act like you don’t. Especially if they're in another language—play clueless.

Ask a million questionsAct like instructions are confusing. Keep asking for clarification or bug your boss constantly, pretending you really want to "get it right."/span>

Do a bad job and blame your toolsMess up your work and blame the tools or equipment. Complain that they're holding you back from doing a good job.

Gatekeep your skillsNever teach new workers what you know. Let them figure it out the hard way.

Mess with paperworkFill out forms so badly that they have to be redone. Miss out key info on purpose.

Form annoying employee groupsJoin or start a group to raise "issues" with management. Make the process complicated, with lots of meetings and unnecessary problems.

Send stuff to the wrong placeMisroute materials or parts just to create chaos.

Mix it upCombine good parts with scrap and rejected stuff so no one knows what’s what.
