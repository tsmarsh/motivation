How to disrupt as a manager

"I need it in writing"Always demand written orders, no exceptions.

Play dumbPretend you don’t get the orders. Ask a million questions or start an email thread that never ends. Nitpick every little thing.

Delay everythingHold off on delivering anything until all parts of an order are ready, even if some parts are already good to go.

Don't restock until it's too lateWait until you're practically out of supplies before you order more. That way, any delay means work comes to a grinding halt.

Go for the Gucci materialsAlways ask for the best stuff that's hard to get. If you don’t get it, complain. Warn everyone that using anything else will mess up the quality.

Give the wrong jobs to the wrong peopleHand out the easy tasks first, then give the hard, important stuff to the people who are least likely to do it well.

Demand perfection on the unimportantBe super picky about things that don’t matter, but ignore real problems in stuff no one can see.

Send things to the wrong placeIntentionally mess up the routing so parts and materials end up where they don’t belong.

Mess up new hiresGive newbies half-baked instructions, making sure they’re confused from the start.

Reward slackers, trash the starsBe extra nice to the workers who suck, even promote them. But for the ones doing a great job? Complain about their work and kill their vibe.

Meetings > workSchedule unnecessary meetings right when there’s real work to be done.

Paperwork overloadMake extra paperwork seem like a must. Start duplicate files, just because.

More hoops to jump throughMake sure at least three people have to sign off on something that could've been approved by just one./span>

Stick to the rules (too much)Follow every single rule to the letter, even when it makes no sense.
